# Brief: "The fake mental math of reply guilt"

**Format:** 6-slide carousel  
**Hook:** "You are not busy. You are emotionally buffering."  
**Status:** Script Ready  
**Visual tone:** Stats-card aesthetic, deadpan infographic, self-aware humor

---

## Concept

Everyone knows the feeling: you've checked your phone 40 times, watched three videos, spiraled on Instagram — and still haven't texted your mom back.  
This carousel names the psychological trick you're playing on yourself. You're not busy. You're doing the math on what replying *costs* and deciding it's too much.  
The app gives you the exit ramp: it makes replying feel like nothing, because it reminds you before the emotional debt compounds.

---

## Slide Breakdown

### Slide 1 — The Hook
**Visual:** Screen time stats card. "You checked your phone 52 times today."  
**Text overlay:** "You have not responded to your mom in 3 days."  
**Tone:** Clinical. Stat card aesthetic. Let the math embarrass you.

### Slide 2 — The Alibi
**Visual:** Split screen — Screen Time bar chart with TikTok, YouTube, Instagram usage highlighted.  
**Text overlay:** "You had time for 2 YouTube rabbit holes, 1 scroll session, and a 22-minute TikTok disappearance."  
**Tone:** Funny because it's specific. Don't soften it.

### Slide 3 — The Diagnosis
**Visual:** Full-bleed text card. Progress bar labeled "EMOTIONALLY BUFFERING..." at 94%.  
**Text overlay:** "You are not busy. You are emotionally buffering."  
**Tone:** This is the punchline slide. Pause here visually.

### Slide 4 — The Cognitive Load Breakdown
**Visual:** Brain diagram (simple illustration or text list) with branching thoughts:  
`→ What do I even say?`  
`→ What if she calls?`  
`→ Now we're in a whole conversation`  
`→ I should wait until I have time to really talk`  
**Text overlay:** "Texting back feels like a commitment when it's actually 12 seconds."  
**Tone:** Self-aware. This is the audience going "wait that's exactly what I do."

### Slide 5 — The Pattern Interrupt
**Visual:** App notification on phone. Low-friction, casual: "Hey — your mom texted 2 days ago 💬"  
**Text overlay:** "Text Your Mom reminds you before the debt compounds."  
**Tone:** The relief slide. Product enters here naturally.

### Slide 6 — The Close
**Visual:** Timer. 0:12. Countdown to zero.  
**Text overlay:** "12 seconds. That's all it takes."  
**Sub-text:** "Download. Get the reminder. Text her back."  
**Tone:** Clean, confident, warm.

---

## Creative Notes

- Slides 1–2: lean into the stats card aesthetic — real-looking screen time data, not mocked-up
- Slide 3 is the hero moment — make the progress bar full-width, high contrast
- Slide 4 works as a simple bulleted list with a brain icon, no need for complex illustration
- Do not use stock family photos anywhere
- The humor comes from the specificity of the cognitive spiral — resist the urge to be vague
- Works without audio; captions carry the joke

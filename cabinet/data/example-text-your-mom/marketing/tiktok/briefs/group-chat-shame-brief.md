# Brief: "Group chat shame spiral"

**Format:** 5-slide carousel (adapted from skit concept)  
**Hook:** "Me opening the family group chat after 4 days of silence"  
**Status:** Research — validate format before scripting  
**Visual tone:** Chat-thread mockup, decision-tree humor, escalating absurdity

---

## Concept

The longer you ignore a group chat, the more impossible it becomes to re-enter it.  
This carousel maps the psychological math of group chat re-entry — and lands on the insight that the app exists to catch you on Day 1, before the stakes get weird.  
Secondary upside: this could anchor a recurring "family group chat" character series if the format lands.

---

## Slide Breakdown

### Slide 1 — The Inventory
**Visual:** Group chat bubble. "Family 💚" with a badge showing "47".  
**Text overlay:** "Me, opening the family group chat after 4 days of silence."  
**Sub-text:** "47 unread messages. Here we go."  
**Tone:** Relatable dread. This is the "before I look" moment.

### Slide 2 — The Contents
**Visual:** Scrolling chat mockup — labeled messages:  
`🙏 — "Prayer emoji"`  
`📸 — "Photo of someone's kid I half-recognize"`  
`😂 — "Meme that's a year old"`  
`🙂 — "Just checking in :)"`  
**Text overlay:** "There is a 🙂 in there. Someone is performing patience."  
**Tone:** The labeled inventory format is funny on its own.

### Slide 3 — The Decision Tree
**Visual:** Simple flowchart / decision tree:  
`Like each photo → "Engagement. I'm here but not talking."`  
`Drop a 'haha' → "I'm committed. We're in it now."`  
`Reply with words → "Now I live here."`  
**Text overlay:** "The stakes of each response type explained."  
**Tone:** Deadpan systems humor. The logic is real.

### Slide 4 — The Compounding Problem
**Visual:** Timeline bar — Day 1: "Easy. Just say hi." → Day 2: "Still fine." → Day 4: "Too late for casual. Need an explanation."  
**Text overlay:** "Day 4 is not a group chat situation. It's a statement."  
**Tone:** The punchline. The math of delay is universal.

### Slide 5 — The Exit Ramp
**Visual:** App notification. "Hey — the family chat is active. Jump in while it's still casual. 💬"  
**Text overlay:** "Text Your Mom catches you on Day 1."  
**Tone:** Clean CTA. The joke resolved with the product as the fix.

## Research Questions Before Scripting

- Does the audience skew more toward "I ignore group chats" vs "I watch the group chat spiral happen to others"? Affects POV framing.
- Test the labeled chat inventory visual — does it read fast enough in carousel format?
- Skit version: could still work as a 15s skit with phone mockup on desk, person nervously tapping. Evaluate after format test.
- Recurring character potential: "Family Group Chat Katie" who always sends the 🙂 — worth noting for future series.

## Creative Notes

- The decision tree in Slide 3 could also work as a meme format (the "two buttons" meme structure)
- Keep the group chat name generic and warm: "Family 💚" not something too specific
- The `🙂` emoji needs to be prominent in Slide 2 — that's the most recognizable passive-aggressive move
- Skit version would need one actor, a phone, a dining table, and good pacing — simple to shoot

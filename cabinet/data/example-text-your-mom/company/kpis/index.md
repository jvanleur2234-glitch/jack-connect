## CFO Financial Commentary (April 13)

**Effective CAC per paying user is ~$223.** Blended CAC of $7.80 divided by 3.5% conversion. At estimated $4.99/month subscription pricing, payback is ~45 months. This is not sustainable.

**Retention is the highest-leverage financial metric.** At 28% W4 retention, most users churn before generating enough revenue to cover acquisition cost. Every point of retention improvement extends average LTV more than any conversion optimization can.

**Content-led organic growth is the only viable acquisition path.** Paid acquisition at current conversion economics destroys value. The marketing cabinets must activate immediately — not as a growth experiment, but as a financial survival requirement.

**The P1 -> P2 -> P3 -> P4 sequence is financially correct.** Activation (P1) increases the pool of users who can convert. Retention (P2, P3) extends the revenue each user generates. Conversion (P4) captures more of that extended revenue.

**RT-4 bug is a financial blocker.** Good to see the CTO started investigating (Apr 13). It gates P2, which gates retention improvement, which gates LTV improvement.

# Brainstorm — April 2026

Raw ideas across product, growth, retention, monetization, and wildcards. Nothing here is committed — this is the idea pile. Items worth pursuing get promoted to [[goals]] or the [[app-development]] backlog.

---

## Product Features

1. **Streak System** — Show a reply streak counter ("You and Mom: 14-day streak"). Loss aversion keeps people texting. Broken streaks get a gentle "It's been 3 days — she probably noticed" nudge.

2. **Conversation Starters** — When a user hasn't replied in 48h, suggest a low-effort opener: "Send her this photo from 2 years ago today" or "Ask her what she had for dinner." Reduces the blank-screen paralysis.

3. **Guilt-O-Meter** — A playful, animated gauge that fills up the longer you ignore someone. Shareable as a screenshot/widget. Part product, part meme format.

4. **Group Nudges** — "The family group chat has been dead for 9 days. You could fix that." Expand beyond 1:1 to group dynamics.

5. **Voice Note Shortcut** — One-tap to record and send a 15-second voice memo through the app. Lower effort than typing when you don't know what to say.

6. **Birthday/Anniversary Auto-Remind** — Import dates from contacts, remind 2 days early so the user isn't the person who texts at 11pm.

7. **"Just Checking In" Templates** — Pre-written messages sorted by relationship (parent, sibling, old friend, ex-coworker). User edits slightly, sends. Removes the "what do I even say" blocker.

8. **Weekly Recap Card** — "This week you replied to 4 people, ignored 2, and your mom texted you twice with no response." Shareable, funny, slightly guilt-inducing.

---

## Growth & Virality

9. **Guilt Trip Shares** — Let users send a friend a link: "Someone added you to Text Your Mom because you're terrible at replying." The recipient downloads the app to see who called them out. Built-in viral loop.

10. **TikTok Sound / Template** — Create an original sound: "POV: You just realized you left your mom on read for 5 days." Encourage duets and stitches. The sound drives app awareness without feeling like an ad.

11. **Reddit AMA / Story Series** — "I built an app because I kept ghosting my mom. AMA." Authentic founder stories on r/relationship_advice, r/ADHD, r/internetparents. These communities eat this up.

12. **Referral Streak Bonus** — Invite a friend → both get a streak shield (one free "streak save" when you miss a day). Costs nothing, drives installs.

13. **"Worst Texter" Leaderboard** — Opt-in leaderboard among friend groups. Nobody wants to be last. Competitive shame as a growth mechanic.

14. **Mother's Day Campaign** (May 11) — "Don't let an app be the reason you finally texted your mom." Anti-marketing that leans into self-awareness. Timely, 4 weeks away.

15. **Campus Ambassador Program** — College students are the worst repliers and the most meme-literate. Give them early access to premium features for posting content.

## Retention & Engagement

16. **Smart Timing** — Learn when each contact is most likely to respond and nudge the user at that window. Replying feels rewarding when you get a response. (Blocked by RT-4 notification bug — see [[app-development]])

17. **Emotional Payoff Moments** — After the user sends a text, show a warm micro-animation: "Your mom is probably smiling right now." Small dopamine hit reinforces the loop.

18. **Relationship Health Score** — A simple score per contact based on reply frequency, initiative, and recency. Gamifies the behavior without being creepy.

19. **Milestone Celebrations** — "You've texted your mom every week for a month." Badge system tied to consistency, not volume.

20. **Dynamic Reminder Tone** — Reminders get funnier and more dramatic the longer you wait. Day 1: "Hey, might want to reply." Day 5: "Your mom has started a support group for parents of terrible texters."

21. **"Reply Debt" Concept** — Visualize how many unreturned messages you have across all contacts. Satisfying to pay it down to zero.

## Monetization

22. **Premium: Custom Reminder Voices** — Celebrity or character voice packs for reminders. "Morgan Freeman reminds you to call your mother." Goofy, premium-worthy.

23. **Premium: Relationship Insights** — Monthly report on your communication patterns across all contacts. Who you're closest to, who's drifting, seasonal patterns.

24. **Premium: Streak Shields** — Free tier gets 1 streak save/month. Premium gets unlimited. Protects the thing users care about most.

25. **Premium: AI Reply Suggestions** — Not just templates but context-aware suggestions based on the last few messages. "Your dad asked about your job interview — here's a follow-up."

26. **Gift Subscriptions** — "Give the gift of guilt." Buy a year of premium for the worst texter in your family. Natural gifting moment for holidays.

27. **Annual Plan with Family Bundle** — $3.99/month solo, $6.99/month for up to 4 family members. Increases retention because canceling affects others.

## Partnerships & Distribution

28. **Therapy App Integration** — Partner with BetterHelp, Talkspace, etc. Relationship maintenance is adjacent to mental health. Cross-promote to their user base.

29. **Phone Carrier Bundles** — T-Mobile, Verizon "family plan" add-on. They already market around family connection.

30. **Apple Watch / Wear OS Complication** — Glanceable guilt. A watch face that shows your reply debt count. Quick-reply from wrist.

31. **iMessage / WhatsApp Extension** — Meet users where they already text. A keyboard extension or share sheet action that brings Text Your Mom features into native messaging.

---

## Wildcards

32. **Expand Beyond "Mom"** — The brand is sticky but limiting. Consider "Text Your People" as the product umbrella, with "Text Your Mom" as the flagship campaign. Same app, broader use case.

33. **Physical Merch** — "World's Worst Texter" mugs, stickers, t-shirts. The brand is inherently merchandise-friendly. Merch doubles as awareness.

34. **Podcast / Short-Form Series** — "Left on Read" — 3-minute episodes of real stories about people who almost lost relationships to bad texting habits. Emotional, shareable, brand-building content.

35. **AI Relationship Coach (Long-term)** — Beyond reminders, an AI that helps navigate difficult conversations, suggests topics to reconnect over, and notices when a relationship is cooling off. The "caring, not nagging" philosophy at scale.

36. **Enterprise / HR Play (Way Later)** — Manager-to-team check-in tool. Same mechanic: nudge, low-effort, reduce distance. Completely different brand but same engine.

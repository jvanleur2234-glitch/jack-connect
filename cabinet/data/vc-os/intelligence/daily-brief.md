## Famous Old AI Tweets (Historical Archive)

### The Early Warning Shots

> **@elonmusk** · August 2014
> "We need to be super careful with AI. Potentially more dangerous than nukes."

Musk's warning came a full year before the open letter on AI safety, a full decade before it entered mainstream political discourse. At the time, most of Silicon Valley laughed. Nobody's laughing now.

---

> **@geoff_hinton** · May 2023
> "I console myself with the normal excuse: If I hadn't done it, someone else would have. It is hard to see how you can prevent this."

Hinton's exit tweet from Google — the godfather of deep learning publicly expressing regret about his life's work. The most consequential resignation in tech history, 280 characters at a time.

---

> **@ylecun** · 2023 (ongoing thread)
> "Auto-regressive LLMs are not the right architecture for human-level AI. They are doomed to hallucinate."

LeCun vs. the world. Meta's chief AI scientist has been consistent (and contrarian) about LLMs' fundamental limits. Still wrong on timing, possibly right on ceiling.

### The Pivotal Predictions

> **@sama** · June 2020 (on GPT-3 launch)
> "The GPT-3 hype is way too much. It's an impressive technical result, but it's a research preview — brittle, limited, and unreliable in practice."

Sam Altman being *right* about GPT-3 in a way that he later had to walk back. AGI claims aged differently than this caution.

---

> **@karpathy** · 2017
> "Software 2.0 is written in neural network weights. We need to build the compilers, debuggers, and IDEs for this new paradigm."

The tweet that launched a thousand YC pitches. Karpathy's "Software 2.0" framing became the intellectual scaffolding for half the developer tooling startups founded 2020–2024 — including, indirectly, the thesis behind DevForge.

---

> **@benedictevans** · 2018
> "Machine learning is the new electricity. Which means it's infrastructure, and everyone in the value chain eventually gets commoditized."

Aged perfectly. Foundation model labs are the utilities. The application layer is where value accumulates.

### The Cultural Moments

> **@waitbutwhy** · March 2016 (on AlphaGo beating Lee Sedol)
> "The most important event in human history is happening right now and nobody's talking about it except Go players."

The AlphaGo moment. Lee Sedol retired from professional Go shortly after, saying a machine could always beat him. One game, four losses, the beginning of the end of "AI can't do X."

---

> **@emostaque** · 2022 (on Stable Diffusion release)
> "We are releasing Stable Diffusion open source today. This is the beginning of AI-generated media as a public utility, not a corporate product."

Open-sourcing the image generation model that started the creative AI debate — copyright, labor displacement, deepfakes — all at once.

> **@fchollet** · 2019
> "AI is a tool. Tools don't have goals. The danger isn't Skynet — it's the humans who will use powerful AI tools to concentrate power."

François Chollet, Keras creator, offering the contrarian take on AI risk that still hasn't been fully disproven: the alignment problem might be a human values problem, not a machine learning one.
